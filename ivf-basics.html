In Vitro Fertilization (IVF)

Our team offers some of the highest IVF success rates in the nation.

Learn More

Solving the infertility puzzle can be a long, discouraging process. IVF offers renewed hope. Our staff will make every effort to provide you with the personal and compassionate care you need during your treatment.

How Does IVF Work?

IVF increases the probability of pregnancy by harnessing the body's own reproductive potential. During a typical menstrual cycle, a hormone called FSH (follicle stimulating hormone) signals to your body to release an egg (oocyte) through a follicle.

Typically one egg is released per cycle, but by giving the body more FSH, more of the oocytes can be cultivated in the hope they will generate a fertilized egg, a good embryo, and ultimately a baby.

Is IVF Right for You?

IVF may be the right option if you've been trying to conceive for six months to a year without success, or if you know you'll need medical support to build your family. It's also commonly used bycouples who haven't had success with other fertility treatments like medicated cycles or IUI.

IVF is often recommended for people navigating challenges such as polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS), endometriosis, low sperm count, or other reproductive health concerns. Whatever your reason for considering IVF, our goal is to meet you where you are and guide you forward with clarity and compassion.

What You Can Expect from the IVF Process

Your IVF journey is personalâ€”and we're here to support you at every step. Here's an overview of what the process typically looks like, so you can feel more prepared and confident moving forward.

Consultation & Testing

Your journey begins with a private one-on-one consultation with a fertility specialist. This is where we'll learn about your medical history, discuss your goals, and begin building a plan that's tailored just for you.

Ovarian Stimulation

You'll take hormone medications for about 10-14 days to encourage your ovaries to develop multiple eggs. Regular monitoring ensures optimal progress.

Egg Retrieval

A short, minimally invasive procedure under anesthesia to collect mature eggs using ultrasound guidance. Same-day discharge with minimal discomfort.

Fertilization & Embryo Development

Eggs are combined with sperm to create embryos. Our embryologists monitor development closely and may recommend genetic testing for optimal selection.

Embryo Transfer

A healthy embryo is gently transferred into your uterus using a thin catheter. Quick, comfortable procedure with no anesthesia required.

The Two-Week Wait

Continue taking prescribed hormones while your body supports implantation. We provide emotional support and guidance during this waiting period.

Pregnancy Test

After the two-week wait, it's time for your pregnancy test. If it's positive, we'll celebrate with you and guide you into prenatal care.
